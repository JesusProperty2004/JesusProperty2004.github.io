Oh Brother! Oh-Brother
Genesis 4
Adam and Eve had 2 sons Cain and Abel. Cain was a farmer and Abel was a shepherd. One day Cain brought, as an offering to God, some fruit and grain that he had grown. Abel, too, brought some of the best animals of his flock and sacrificed their fat pieces to God. God was pleased with Abel and his offering, but Cain and his offering did not please him. Cain was mad that God didn't like his offering. Cain grumbled with anger and scowled with turmoil. "Why are you angry and why do you scowl? If you do what is right, your offering will be accepted? But if you do wrong, sin enters like a wild animal at the door and the desire to sin will overcome you."
Cain said to his brother Abel, "Let us go into the field." And while they were in the field, Cain struck his brother Abel and killed him.
God asked about Abel, Cain was mad, he didn't want people to think he was supposed to keep track of him. When God found out Cain killed his own brother, he told Cain he shall accept his punnishments.
